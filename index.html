Who We Are

We are a group of students from University of Washington who were made aware of the challenges that the gender binary poses for people through a presentation given in our Informatics 200 class. Currently, the gender binary is the most dominant depiction of gender in society. However, not everyone identifies with these binaries, thus causing conflict within themselves on how to express themselves. People who don’t adhere to the gender binary have a hard time expressing themselves for fear of being judged or discriminated against. We wanted to do something about this issue.

Our Goal

We want to educate the public about the gender spectrum. This would make them less judgemental towards those who don’t adhere to the binary system. Along with them being less judgmental, we’d want others to feel comfortable with themselves. We want the non-binary genders to express themselves freely.

We want to bring awareness to the problem that the gender binary creates in society, whilst also providing support for those who are just beginning to transition or even question themselves outside of the parameters of the binary. We are attempting to remove the impact of stigmas on trans/questioning individuals, and thus enable themselves to live their lives with fewer perceived restrictions. By better educating people about the gender spectrum, we can help mitigate the cycle of inequality against those who do not adhere to the gender binary. Furthermore, we want to provide trans/questioning people with support in the form of online resources as well as tangible evidence of support.

Donate

There are currently two ways in which you can become a patron. You can donate your gently used clothing or anything else you think would be beneficial by providing your name, email address, and shipping address so that we can contact you and send you a prepaid shipping bag for your items. Or if you wish to, we also accept monetary donations via PayPal that are used to cover the costs of shipping and buying new clothes to include in the packages. Either way is greatly appreciated. Patrons can also rest assured that your information is safe and secure and will not be used in any other way than which you intended.






Don't have items to donate but still want to help? Patrons can donate funds via paypal. Any funds donated to us will be used for shipping labels, purchasing items in high demand, as well as advertising so we can help more people.

Receive the Gift of Comfort

If you feel as if you could benefit from our help, don’t be afraid to request a package. After a short questionnaire designed to give us an idea of what to send you, we request that you provide your name, email address, shipping address, and phone number. After submitting the form, you will get a confirmation pin via text message that you will have to enter in order to confirm your request. The gift will contain several pieces of clothing for you. If you have any special requests, such as specific types of clothing you want, feel free to describe them in the textbox below.

Let us help you

Below are some helpful links to learn more about gender identity and the gender spectrum. Forums and hotlines are available if you need to talk to someone about an issue pertaining to gender identity.



Organizations

Center for Excellence for Transgender Health aims to increase access to comprehensive, effective, and affirming health care services for transgender and gender-variant communities.

COLAGE unites people with lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and/or queer parents into a network of peers and supports them as they nurture and empower each other to be skilled, self-confident, and just leaders in our collective communities.

Gender Spectrum provides education, training, and support to help create a gender-sensitive and inclusive environment for all children and teens. Gender Spectrum also offers a wide range of resources, training and consultation to support schools to be more welcoming for all students, regardless of their gender.

The National Center for Transgender Equality (NCTE) is dedicated to advancing the equality of transgender people through advocacy, collaboration, and empowerment.

Online Communities

Parents of Transgender Kids was founded in 2011, a forum for sharing information, resources, and support among parents (current membership of 1300) with transgender children and teens.

Transfamily based out of Cleveland, Ohio, Transfamily is a support network created by the parents of a transgender person. The website includes free discussion boards in the following categories: all-inclusive, parent-specific, couple-specific, spouse-specific, youth-specific, children-specific.

Tri-Ess is an educational, social and support group for heterosexual crossdressers, their partners, the spouses of married crossdressers and their families.

The Lounge is the Gender Spectrum’s online community serving teens, parents, and professionals.

General Information and Resources

Transgender Lives: Your Stories is an interactive campaign created by The New York Times to allow transgender people to tell their own stories in their own words.

The Trans 100 is an annual list of 100 amazing and inspiring trans Americans who are visible within their communities.

I AM: Trans People Speak is a campaign created by the Massachusetts Transgender Political Coalition (MTPC) and sponsored by GLAAD to raise awareness about the diversity of transgender communities. It lifts the voices of transgender individuals, as well as their families, friends, and allies.

Trans Student Equality Resrouces (TSER) provides trans*-related information on school issues and supports efforts for creating policy change in school districts.

Transgender people in crisis should contact the following resources:

The Trevor Project's 24/7 Lifeline at 866-4-U-TREVOR (866-488-7386) or Trevor Chat, the Trevor Project's online messaging service

The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 800-273-TALK (8255)

Trans Lifeline at 877-565-8860
